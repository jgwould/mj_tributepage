Michael Jordan

"I've missed more than 9000 shots in my career. I've lost almost 300 games. 26 times, I've been trusted to take the game winning shot and missed. I've failed over and over and over again in my life. And that is why I succeed."

Michael Jordan at home in Chicago during the 1988 Slam Dunk contest, where, with this dunk, he defeated Dominique Wilkins to defend the Slam Dunk crown.

Here's a timeline of Jordan's life:

  • 1963 - Michael Jordan is born in Brooklyn, New York; family relocates to Wilmington, NC
  • 1978 - Jordan goes out for the Varsity basketball team as a sophomore, but he doesn't make it
  • 1979 - Jordan works really hard over the summer and grows 4 inches
  • 1981 - During his senior year, Jordan averages 29 points 11 rebounds and 10 assists
  • 1981 - He is heavily recruited by many powerhouse basketball schools but he decides to attend the University of North Carolina
  • 1982 - As a frehman, Jordan drains a game winning shot in the NCAA chamionship against Patrick Ewing and Georgetown; Jordan would later go on to say that this feat was a major turning point in his basektball career
  • 1982 - Awarded Atlantic Coast Conference Freshman of the Year Award
  • 1983 - First Team NCAA All-American
  • 1984 - First Team NCAA All American
  • 1984 - Won the Naismith and the Wooden College Player of the Year awards
  • 1984 - Michael leaves UNC a year early to enter the NBA draft; The Chicago Bulls draft him with the third overall pick
  • Won the gold medal in the 1984 Summer Olympics with Team USA
  • 1985 - NBA Rookie of the Year
  • 1988 - NBA Defensive Player of the Year
  • 1991 - Led the Chicago Bulls to their first ever NBA title while being crowned Finals MVP
  • 1992 - Led the Bulls to their second NBA title while acheiving MVP honors averaging over 35 points per game
  • 1992 - Won the gold medal in the 1992 Summer Olympics as a part of the infamous USA Basketball "Dream Team"
  • 1993 - Led Chicago to their third straight NBA title while being crowned Finals MVP with averaging 41 points per game
  • 1993 - MJ retires from basketball to pursue baseball
  • 1995 - After an unsuccessful baseball career, Jordan returns to basketball
  • 1996 - Jordan led the Bulls to their fourth NBA Finals title while achieving NBA MVP honors for the fourth time averaging 27 points
  • 1997 - MJ led the Bulls to their fifth NBA title while attaining the Finals MVP award averaging 32 points
  • 1998 - In Jordan's last season with the Bulls, he led his team to their sixth NBA title while again achieving NBA Finals MVP trophy averaging 33 points
  • 1998 - Jordan retires for a second time from the NBA
  • 2001-2003 - Comes out of retirement again to play with the Washington Wizards for two seasons
  • 2006- present - Managing member of Basketball Operations for the Charlotte Hornets
